Who am I?

I'm a passionate scientist dedicated to exploring the mysteries of the world we live in. I made this website to be a small scientific corner for me to share the research I've been doing for the last decade as well as any future research or experiments that I'll be doing.

I specialize in computer security & love science in general. I'm known for my out of the box thinking which I use to solve problems & formulating hypotheses. My experiments are generally based upon simple questions or driven by the curiosity to understand a natural behavior or even sometimes a simple goal. However, an important detail is I always try to keep my experiments replicable & ensure that it's as unbiased as possible to ensure I follow the doctrine of science as much as I can. This is mainly due to the fact that I wish to one day publish some research I conducted in the scientific community papers so that I may contribute in a more formal way. Experiments that are done without following the principles of science might be fun, but they aren't as satisfying to me.

I also wrote some articles on Omerta Security's blog in the past which pertained to Tor & PHP. I've spent the better part of the last decade learning about computer security, programming, computer science & anything I could get my hands on that was related to electronics with a dash of biology, chemistry, physics, mathematics as well as anything else that I stumbled upon.
